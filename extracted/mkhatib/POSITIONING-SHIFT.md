# Positioning Shift · v3 (Civilian Edition)

The brand kit went through three iterations. This document captures all three so the reasoning is preserved.

---

## v1 — written under uncertainty

The first kit was built before the CV arrived. We knew the website existed, the patents existed, the academic affiliation existed. We did not know what the rest looked like, so v1 hedged. It said *patents are Lebanese-registered, not international*. It said *demote SPSC to footnote*. It said *no military precision tagline unless LAF affiliation is confirmed*.

The positioning v1 produced: **"a solo Beirut consulting practice in structural, geotechnical, and forensic civil engineering."**

---

## v2 — written under abundance

The 17-page CV arrived. Substantially larger record than v1 had assumed: 52 publications with DOIs, 21 editorial board positions, 2 Lebanese-registered patents, a published book on Amazon, plus a documented military career and four committee chairmanships.

v2 expanded the kit to honour that abundance — added a publications page (all 52 papers, sortable), an editorial-roles page (all 21 positions), a public-service-record page (the four committee chairmanships in heritage-green register), a fourth pillar (Disaster & Post-Event Assessment).

But every LAF mention was wrapped in an HTML comment marked "pending Jesse's WhatsApp confirmation with client before publish." The kit was built to handle either consent state — civilian-only, or LAF-included.

The positioning v2 produced: **"Lebanese engineering officer, academic, and published researcher — patents, peer-reviewed publications, expert assessment, and a 26-year career across the LAF Engineering Directorate and Lebanese universities."**

---

## v3 — Dr. Khatib confirmed: civilian only

On 2 May 2026, Dr. Khatib confirmed via WhatsApp:

> "Yes... kindly, I don't like to mention about military personality."

That preference is final and applies everywhere on every public surface. The active war and his serving status make this the obviously correct call. v3 strips every military reference from the kit and adjusts positioning accordingly.

## What changed v2 → v3

### 1. Three pillars, not four

The fourth pillar — Disaster & Post-Event Assessment — was tied to the public-service committee work. Without that work referenced, the fourth pillar has no grounding. v3 returns to the original three pillars: Structural, Geotechnical, Forensic.

### 2. The public-service-record page is held back

Even with all explicit LAF references stripped, the pattern of being committee chief at every major Lebanese disaster between 2008 and 2020 is identifying for anyone who knows how Lebanese disaster-response committees are constituted. The page is preserved in `/_optional` for possible future use; it does not run in the active kit.

### 3. The MBA-in-progress is now part of the academic record

Per Dr. Khatib's WhatsApp message, an MBA from AUST is expected by end of semester. v3 references it as "MBA Candidate, AUST" — never as already-conferred until he confirms the conferral.

### 4. Language certificates simplified

The CV documents formal language certificates from the French Ministry of Defense, the Italian Cultural Institute, and others. v3 keeps the languages (English, French, Italian, Arabic native) but does not name certifying bodies that are themselves military.

### 5. The AUST Strategic and Security Analysis diploma is excluded

It's listed in the CV but its name is military-policy adjacent. v3 substitutes the MBA-in-progress as the post-PhD academic credential.

### 6. The Beirut Port reference is gone

In v2 the 2020 Beirut Port damage assessment was the heaviest line in the public-service record. v3 removes it entirely. The port is one of the most identifying possible references — anyone in Lebanese engineering knows who chaired that committee.
