## 0. Hard Constraints — These Hold Across Everything

These are non-negotiable. Every future deliverable, prompt, designer, freelancer, or AI build must respect them.

1. **Zero military references. Anywhere. Ever.**
   Dr. Khatib is active and there is war. No mention of armed forces, defense engineering, military rank, military structures, uniformed photos, or any euphemism for it (e.g. "national service," "defense sector"). Audit must include:
   - The new site copy
   - The downloadable CV PDF (currently linked from Google Drive — **must be re-rendered without military line items**)
   - LinkedIn personal profile
   - LinkedIn company page
   - Any AI-generated bio that pulled from older sources
   - Speaking gallery captions (some conferences may have been military-adjacent — verify each)

2. **Thought leader, not service provider.** No "Services" dropdown. No procurement language. No rate-card energy.

3. **Headline lock:**
   > Dr. Milad Khatib | Civil Engineer & System Thinker | Architecting Resilient Futures through Precision & Innovation

   Current site uses "Building Tomorrow's Infrastructure with Expert Precision" — generic SaaS phrasing. Replace.

4. **First person voice.** Current site is third person ("Dr. Khatib is an accomplished…"). Rewrite to "I."

5. **EN + AR parity.** Arabic is a peer language, not a translation afterthought. RTL handled properly.

6. **Three pillars in order:** academic rigor → global expertise → practical innovation.

## 2. The Principle Shift

Right now the site answers: **"What does Dr. Khatib do?"**
The legendary version answers: **"What does Dr. Khatib *believe*, and what's the 26-year evidence trail?"**

That single shift restructures everything:

| Service-provider page | Thought-leader page |
|---|---|
| Hero CTA: "Schedule Consultation" | Hero: a thesis statement, in his voice |
| About → bio paragraph | About → first-person essay, ~200 words |
| Services grid (6 cards) | Practice areas, framed as ongoing inquiries |
| Patents in a list | Patents as missions, problem-first |
| Speaking gallery, generic captions | Selected talks, with arguments |
| Contact → Service Interest dropdown | Contact → "What are you working on?" |
| No essays | Essays / op-eds — the proof of the thesis |
| English only | EN ↔ AR toggle, full parity |

The work product (engineering consulting, expert testimony, academic collaboration) doesn't disappear — it sits *one click in*, where serious clients find it. The homepage is for the argument.

## 4. Visual Identity Direction

We don't have a Milad Khatib brand kit yet. We need one. Direction:

### Typography
- **Display:** an editorial serif with weight and presence — *Cormorant Garamond* or *Instrument Serif*. Reads as academic, not corporate.
- **Body:** a humanist sans — *Inter Tight* or *IBM Plex Sans*. Calm, technical.
- **Arabic:** *IBM Plex Sans Arabic* or *Noto Naskh Arabic* — designed as peers to the Latin set, not retrofitted.
- **Numerals/data:** *JetBrains Mono* for any technical specs, patent numbers, dates.

### Palette
Move off v0 default greys. Proposed:
- **Ink** — deep near-black for body text
- **Stone** — warm bone/cream as page background (not stark white — gives weight)
- **Slate** — a deep blue for structural emphasis
- **Sand** or **Copper** — a single warm accent, used sparingly for active elements

The combination should feel like a serious quarterly journal, not a SaaS landing page.

### Grid
Break the v0 card-grid sameness. Use editorial layouts — generous whitespace, asymmetric breaks, full-bleed photography in select moments. Each section reads as if typeset, not assembled.

### Photography
The portrait and speaking shots already exist. They need:
- Color grading consistency (currently mixed)
- Cropping discipline (some are landscape, some portrait — pick a system)
- Real captions (see Section G above)

### EN ↔ AR Treatment
- Toggle in masthead, persists across navigation
- RTL layout properly implemented (not just text-direction flipped — the *grid* should mirror)
- Arabic typography given the same care as Latin — no defaulting to Times New Roman fallback

## 7. The "Stand Way Out" Test

Before anything ships, every page passes this check:

1. **Three-second test.** A serious visitor lands. Within three seconds, they should know: *this person believes something specific about how infrastructure should be built, and has decades of evidence.*
2. **Voice test.** Read any paragraph aloud. If it sounds like a publicist or a brochure, it's wrong. If it sounds like Milad in conversation, it's right.
3. **Subtraction test.** Could this section exist on a thousand other engineer portfolios? If yes, cut it or sharpen it until the answer is no.
4. **Bilingual test.** Is the Arabic doing equal work? If it's a translation afterthought, it's not done.
5. **Safety test.** Zero military surface — across copy, photos, captions, CV, metadata, alt text, and any embedded PDFs.
