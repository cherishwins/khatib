REF  MK-2026-039   ·   DATE  02 May 2026   ·   RE  Forensic Structural Opinion — South Beirut Tower

Dear Counsel,

Following the site inspection of 27 April 2026 and review of the original post-tension shop drawings, our finite-element re-analysis of slab levels 18 through 22 confirms the corner-deflection pattern reported in the developer’s 90-day monitoring data. Total measured deflection at column line C4-C5 reached 38 mm, exceeding the L/240 serviceability limit prescribed by ACI 318 and the Lebanese seismic provisions.

Three findings are material to your action. First, the failure mode is consistent with monostrand anchorage slip at the boundary tendons, a phenomenon I have characterised in peer-reviewed work (Heliyon, 2024). Second, the inverted-U reinforcement detail required by the original structural specification appears to have been omitted at three of the affected gridlines. Third, the in-place concrete cylinder strength averaged 28.4 MPa against a 35 MPa specification.

A full technical report, with calculations, photographs, and proposed remediation, will follow under separate cover within ten business days. I am available for deposition by prior arrangement and will testify in English or Arabic.
