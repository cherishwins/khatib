## ◆ &nbsp; Two languages — how the toggle works

Every page has a language toggle (usually top‑right, marked **EN / ع**).

- Click it once to flip to the other language
- The toggle is smart: if you're on `miladkhatib.com/about`, it takes you to `miladkhatib.com/ar/about` — the same page, in Arabic
- The Arabic version reads right‑to‑left automatically. Numbers stay in Western digits (0–9) in both languages, by your direction

**Which language do search engines find?**

Both. Google and Bing will index both versions. If someone searches for "Dr. Milad Khatib" in Arabic, they'll land on the Arabic version. If they search in English, they'll land on the English version. You don't have to manage this.

## ◆ &nbsp; The contact form — where messages go

When someone fills out the contact form on `/contact`:

1. They pick a topic (Structural, Geotechnical, Forensic, Patent, Academic, Other)
2. They type their name, email, and message
3. They press **Send**
4. **The message is emailed directly to your inbox.** That's it. No middleman, no portal to log into, no missed notifications.

**Things you should know:**

- The form has a **honeypot** that catches automated spam silently. Real people get through; bots don't. You won't see spam from this form.
- It has a **rate limit**: the same person can only send three messages every ten minutes. This stops anyone from flooding your inbox.
- If the email service ever has a problem, the sender sees a polite error. They don't think their message vanished.
- The reply‑to is set to the sender's email, so when you hit "Reply" in your inbox, your reply goes back to them — not to the website.

**You don't have to log in to anything to read messages.** They arrive in your normal email.

## ◆ &nbsp; Your CV page — how to save it as a PDF

The CV page is at `miladkhatib.com/cv` (and `miladkhatib.com/ar/cv` for Arabic).

It's designed so that when you press **Print** in your browser, it produces a clean, single‑document PDF — no website navigation, no visual clutter, just your CV on plain pages.

**To save as PDF:**

1. Go to `miladkhatib.com/cv`
2. Press **Ctrl+P** on Windows, or **⌘+P** on Mac, or use the browser's File → Print menu
3. In the print dialog, change the destination to **"Save as PDF"** (or "Microsoft Print to PDF")
4. Press Save

You can do this in either English or Arabic. The Arabic version saves an Arabic PDF.

**Why this matters:** when a journal or a client asks for "your CV in PDF," you don't need to email anyone. You just visit the page, save, and send. Always current.

## ◆ &nbsp; The civilian‑only rule — why it matters

You confirmed via WhatsApp on **2 May 2026** that no military reference should appear on any public materials. This direction is final and is enforced at multiple layers:

- Every page of the website
- Every social card (the preview image that shows when someone shares a link on LinkedIn or WhatsApp)
- Every meta description that appears in Google results
- The CV page, the patents pages, the publications list — everything

**There is a built‑in audit.** Every time the website is rebuilt, a script scans every file for banned terms (`military`, `army`, `battalion`, `medal`, `valour`, etc.). If any term is found, the build is blocked and the change cannot go live. You are protected from accidental slips by automation, not just by goodwill.

**What this means in practice:**

- The civilian engineering record alone is the brand
- The 52 publications, 21 editorial seats, 2 patents, 27 years in OEA Beirut — these speak for themselves
- If a journalist or biographer ever asks why your military service is not on the site: it is your stated preference, on record, with date, and the consultancy chooses to honour it without commentary

## ◆ &nbsp; How updates happen — the WhatsApp loop

You don't need to log into anything to update the site. The flow is intentionally simple:

```
   You                 →   WhatsApp message      →   The developer
   ↓                                                         ↓
   "add my new paper                                  Edits the file,
    in Frontiers"                                     pushes to GitHub
                                                              ↓
                                                        Live in 1–2 min
   ↑                                                         ↓
   You see the update in your browser  ←  Confirmed back to you
```

**What kinds of updates take this path:**

- A new publication, with the DOI
- A new editorial board appointment
- An updated photo
- A new talk or keynote
- A change to a service description
- A correction (a typo, a date)
- A new patent
- An MBA conferral confirmation
- A new phone number, an OEA registration number, etc.

**Turnaround:** usually within minutes if the developer is at a keyboard. Worst case, the same day.

**Costs:** updates of this kind are part of the site's regular maintenance. You don't get billed per change.

**One thing to know:** the website is generated from data files. When a publication is added, the system automatically:
- Updates the `/publications` page in both languages
- Re‑sorts entries by year
- Refreshes the topic distribution chart
- Regenerates the social‑share images
- Re‑submits the sitemap to Google

You do nothing for any of this. You send the citation. The rest is automated.

## ◆ &nbsp; Things that look incomplete but are intentional

If you visit your patents page, you may see this:

> **Patent registration number:** `[ to be inserted ]`

That `[ to be inserted ]` is **not a bug**. It's a deliberate placeholder. The brand kit you signed off on includes a rule — **"every claim is traceable"** — and rather than guess a patent number, the website is honest that the value is pending.

The same pattern applies to:

- The 2025 patent's companion publication (says "forthcoming")
- The MBA (says "in progress")
- A few publication entries marked "[VERIFY]" — this means we have the citation but the publisher's link couldn't be machine‑verified at build time; the fact is correct, the live URL just needs a click

**As soon as you supply the missing values, the placeholders disappear.** Until then, the site is honest about what it doesn't know — which protects your reputation more than a guess would.

## ◆ &nbsp; The studio — your private workshop

The **studio** is a private workshop area being built into your website right now. It's the place where you, personally, can compose two kinds of official documents in your brand voice and produce them as polished, print‑ready PDFs — without going through the developer for every single one.

**Two kinds of documents the studio will produce:**

| Document type | What it's for | The output |
|:--------------|:--------------|:-----------|
| **Letter** | A formal letter to a client, a journal editor, a ministry, a contractor — anyone who'd benefit from a properly letter‑headed reply rather than a plain email | A single‑page A4 PDF on your full letterhead — bilingual where helpful |
| **Capability Statement** | A short, sharp document that introduces your practice for a specific kind of work — e.g. "Capability Statement: Forensic Engineering for Insurance Disputes" or "Capability Statement: Geotechnical Investigation for Lebanese Municipalities" | A 1–2 page A4 PDF on your full letterhead, with the relevant pillars and citations pulled in automatically |

**The flow you'll experience, when it's ready:**

1. You'd visit a private URL (not linked from anywhere public — only you know it)
2. You'd type your email
3. You'd receive a one‑time sign‑in link in your inbox (no password to remember, link expires in 15 minutes)
4. You'd click the link and land in the workshop
5. You'd choose **Letter** or **Capability Statement**, and a brand‑perfect template appears with placeholders
6. You'd write or edit the body — in English, Arabic, or both. The language toggle works inside the workshop.
7. When you're satisfied, you'd press **Seal**. This locks the document, applies your signature/seal, records that it was sealed (so you have a log), and produces the PDF.
8. You'd download the PDF or send it directly from the workshop.

**Important details that the workshop will get right:**

- Time is shown in **Beirut time**, not the server's time zone — so the dashboard greeting and timestamps reflect *your* day, not Frankfurt's.
- All animations respect the operating system's "reduce motion" setting — important for accessibility and for anyone reading on a sensitive screen.
- Drafts auto‑save while you write. If your browser closes, your draft is still there.
- Templates live as separate files in the codebase — so refining the language of a Capability Statement template is a focused review, not a sprawling code change.
- The whole workshop is being built with proper tests so the next change doesn't break the previous one.

**Status as of today:**

The workshop is being rebuilt right now to a higher bar than the first prototype. Until it's signed off and live, **the WhatsApp loop described above still handles everything** — including any letters or capability statements you need urgently. You'll know the workshop is ready because you'll receive a single WhatsApp from the developer with the private URL and a short walk‑through.
